From circus performer to award-winning filmmaker

Alaa "Regash" Aliabdallah is a Palestinian photographer, filmmaker and cinematographer based in Nablus, Palestine. Beginning his creative journey in circus performance with the Palestinian Circus School, he transitioned to photography in 2014 and has since built a career spanning documentary filmmaking, fashion photography, and creative direction.

As a freelance photographer, he has documented performances for the Palestinian Circus School, Stereo48 Dance Company, The Freedom Theatre, and Sarreyet Ramallah since 2015, and shot photography for the award-winning film 200 Meters (Venice 2020). His debut feature documentary Palestine Comedy Club, co-directed with Charlotte Knowles, premiered at SXSW London and won the Spirit of Raindance Award — he personally shot all six comedy performances and five interviews, weaving his street photography into cinematic collage stories of each Palestinian city visited on the tour.

The Story

Six Palestinian stand-up comedians cross checkpoints and borders to perform in theatres across Palestine. When they're invited to London, their first performance falls on October 7th, 2023 — just as war breaks out at home. Their mission to connect audiences through laughter becomes an existential imperative.

"The ability to find and share laughter in the darkest of circumstances becomes a powerful act of resistance and hope."
